What Is Speedrunning?

In short: it's completing a video game as fast as possible. Speedrunning is all about beating games in record time through pixel-perfect performance and meticulous research of game mechanics.

But that's not it! Speedrunning also represents a vibrant community that has grown and evolved greatly over the years. It is a prime example of an Internet-native discourse community, a unique phenomenon with many interesting characteristics.

Background: Super Mario Bros. 1 in 4:54 by Niftski

Origins of Speedrunning

The origins of the modern speedrunning community can be traced back to Quake, widely considered to be one of the most influential works in the genre of first-person shooters. Quake was special in that it allowed users to record their inputs during gameplay, producing a "demo" that could be replayed later. This provided a means for gamers to share their playthroughs in an era where low-speed dialup Internet was the norm. Sites dedicated to tracking of records and sharing of demos quickly emerged, and the nascent speedrunning scene was born.

Core Values

In "Code is Law", Michael Hemmingsen argues that the fundamental ethos of speedrunning consists of three principles: skill, knowledge, and subversion. Skill is exemplified by the emphasis placed on technical displays of mastery, knowledge drives open collaboration, and subversion serves as the motivation behind continued exploration.

The speedrun in the background is an excellent example of all three ideas at work. The game is Portal 2, a puzzle platformer where the player must navigate space using portals. Yet through collaboration and careful execution, speedrunners have managed to beat the game without ever entering a portal, entirely subverting the developer's intentions.
